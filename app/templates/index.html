Introduction

Our team has dedicated our time to put together an AI Computer Vision program that detects the style of a specific clothing based on it's accuracy. The majority of this project was created with the help of Cocalc , Roboflow and WandB. Our model is capable of identifying 5 different types of clothing styles and designs. Them being: Casual, Formal, Streetwear, Athletic, and Other. Yolo V5 was also a big contributor to our project success.Yolo V5 is a family of object detection architectures.


Confusion Matrix


A confusion matrix is a heat map that shows the predictions made by the machine compared to the actual target value. It allows people to visualize predictive analytics such as accuracy, recall, and precision. Additionally, confusion matrices provide direct comparisons for true positives, true negatives, false positives, and false negatives. Because the confidence level of our model is set at 0.25, even though our model is good at accurately detecting images, it does not show up on the confusion matrix if the confidence is under 25%.

F1 Curve


F1 score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall that ranges from 0 to 1. The higher the F1 score the better because it is a measure of accuracy. In our F1 curve, the score is the highest at 0.82 when the confidence level is at 14%. As the confidence level rises, the F1 score decreases because the recall decreases due to false negatives.




mAP


mAP (mean Average Precision) is a popular metric for measuring the accuracy of object detectors. A crucial part of a mAP is the IoU (Intersection over Union) which is a measurement of how accurate a predicted bounding box is in comparison to the actual ground truth bounding box. mAP_0.5 means that the IoU threshold is 0.5.







Future Improvements Needed

Hover Mouse Over Button to View

Larger Dataset

Our model isn't perfect. It doesn't accurately predict all clothing styles, and even when it does, its confidence levels aren't that high. Therefore, adding more data and images to train with would help further improve this product.

Precise Annotations

Similar to our dataset, our annotations weren't completely accurate nor precise. Annotations from image to image aren't always consistent (polygons and rectangles). additionally, some of our training image's annotations didn't include an individual's shoes, whilst others did.

Real World Application

Our products abilities are very limited, and can only identify a clothing style. If we create a more robust AI model this program could be used to identify clothing for consumers. Our website would be able to easily identify clothes and redirect the consumer to another website to purchase said clothes.
